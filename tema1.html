5 Robots and Sitemap

Sitemaps are files that provide information about the pages, videos, and other files on your site, and the relationships between them. Search engines, such as Google, read these files to crawl sites more efficiently.
Sitemaps tell us which files on a site are important to you, and also include important information about them. For example, in the case of pages, they can indicate when they were last updated and if they have versions in other languages.

A robots.txt file tells search engine crawlers which URLs on your site they can access. Mainly, it is used to prevent the requests your site receives from overloading it; it is not a mechanism to prevent a web page from appearing on Google.
If you want a web page to not appear in Google, block indexing with noindex or protect the page with a password.
